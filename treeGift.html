Tree Gift App

Product Design

Overview

Trella Tree Gift is a tree gifting app designed for an online tree buying, gifting, and planting experience. It targets people who are environmentally cautious and want to give unique presents to others. Through efforts to conduct user interviews, gain expertise advice, and collect data from iTrees, the app includes features such as naming the tree, building a personal forest, checking tree diaries, and gifting to friends.

Timeline: 3 Months

Tools: Adobe XD

Context: Startup Project

Context

In the Fall of 2020, I joined Trella’s technology solutions team as a product manager and designer. I was asked to design the user journey and experience of a tree gifting App. Trella is a New York based urban forestry company that has over 100k+ trees internationally. As a result, in 3 months, I defined my target user’s needs and designed an app from the ground up. This app’s goal is to inspire people to learn more about trees, shop trees, give trees as presents, share with others tree’s benefits, and track individual contributions.

Solution

Shopping

The home page has a tree shopping area with different filters. Users can view trees with special meanings such as friendship or anniversary. Each tree item provides information about the tree’s eco-benefits, values, and price.

Facts

Each tree’s detail page provides facts about the tree and the values associated with it. Some trees have special meanings while others have bigger environmental benefits. The app is also built for educational purposes.

Gifting

The app offers users a choice to package the tree gift with a gift card section where users can select a background and add a unique message.

Forest

The virtual forest page lets users view the trees they have collected and their total contribution to the environment with 4 cumulative eco-benefit values.

Tree Diary

Users can see updates from their trees in which they also have the naming rights. They may also interact with the posts by clicking the like button.

Process

PROBLEM

Problems that a tree gift app can solve
Environmental Concerns

Climate change is a pressing and serious problem troubling everyone. Many people hope to contribute but don't understand how to help in a time efficient and fun way. Others struggle with finding ways to contribute and inspire more to join the efforts. Our team hope to make a sustainable, accessible, and fun way of planting trees. With further research, I realized it’s also important to educate users on the benefits of trees, and make the online gifting experience exciting so that people feel happy about giving or receiving a tree gift that they can name and grow on the platform.

RESEARCH

Understanding the target users
People who love nature & plants

Our team believes people who buy trees as gifts for themselves or others are the users of American Forest and Treedom. However both of the websites mentioned previously don't offer user experience that helps the users influence their friends to also join the initiative.

People who like gifts

Trees have special meanings. Some trees live to hundreds of years and is often seen as a symbol for longevity. Each tree has a different representation and can have different meanings. People who receive virtual tree gifts will know that a real tree is planted on their behalf. In addition, the online gift opening experience will help people connect with each other through trees.

Value of Purchase

Feedback: Interviewees think there is not enough information on the user experience of the app once they make a purchase.

Solution: We added a short 30-second video in the landing page prior to purchase in order to show users how to use the app. The video includes both how the tree is growing in real life and the user experience on the app. In the future, we will also include gamification features such as watering the tree.

Purpose of App

Feedback: Interviewees who were already familiar with the concept suggested they like how tree facts and environmental benefits are embedded in the app. However, survey responses suggest there needs to be more explanation on why people should purchase.

Solution: In the new prototype, we added texts and images on the home page that helps users to understand the purpose of the app.

REFLECTION
